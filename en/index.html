Public Transport ... what?

Descriptions for many Public Transport Networks (transport associations) can be found in the OSM-Wiki, including a lists of the provided services for Train, Subway, Tram, ..., Bus lines.
Examples: Bus Routes in London, UK, France, RATP, Portland, OR - Transit Routes, Munich, Germany (MVV) (deleted in the meantime though).

It turned out, at least for Munich (MVV), that most of those tables were never up-to-date.

This was motivation for the Munich OSM-Pub-Meeting to discuss and solve the problem.

PTNA - Public Transport Network Analysis now creates those lists and tables on a regular basis and also performs a target/actual analysis.

  • "Target" = what the local transport association offers (based on a CSV list, created and maintained by local OSM-Mappers),
  • "Actual" = what we can find in the OSM Database.

Besides simply producing a list of the routes, an error analysis for the routes and route-masters is done.

Currently, the OSM data of more than 100 transport associations is analysed by PTNA. Adding another transport association to the analysis is simple and can be done quite quickly.

The documentation has just started, in German first, English is on it's way - with lots of 'plase holders' though. Please be patient.
